Muddus National Park

Here you can walk through old-growth forests and bogs where there are bogs and bogs. There is also a bit more dramatic nature such as steep rock ravines and the 42 meter high Muddusagah tent roof / Muddusfallet. In the southern part of the national park, the Muttosälven river flows into the Stora Luleälv.

Dalbo Söderskog

The national park is most beautiful in the spring. The intense, almost exotic, spring flowering with carpets in yellow and white is a welcome sign of spring. Then it's time to bring the blanket and coffee basket for this year's first picnic.

Map-Dalbo Söderskog
Trestickland National Park

From the air, the area resembles a washboard. Nature is characterized by narrow and high ridges that extend in a north-south direction. Between them are bogs and long, narrow lakes. The sparse and barren pine forest with lichen and moss-covered rocks, together with the large number of lakes form a tranquil environment where you can hike for several days.

Map-Trestickland
Björnlandets National Park

Björnlandet National Park was founded in 1991 and consists of 2300 hectares of forest that have been protected as a heritage for our descendants. The forests are similar to the nature that Linnaeus describes from his trip to Lapland just over 280 years ago and consists mostly of old pine. Tree ages around 180 years are most common, but there are also trees that are significantly older than that.

Djurö

The main island Djurö in the far north is around 150 hectares. With its natural harbors, Djurö is a well-known and attractive destination for boat-borne outdoor life in Lake Vänern.

Map-Djurö
Tyresta National Park

Just 20 km from the city of millions, Stockholm, you can experience a really old forest. In Tyresta you can pick berries and edible mushrooms, but not flowers and other plants. At the same time as Tyresta is close to the big city and is a popular nature area, the forests offer a feeling of wilderness.
