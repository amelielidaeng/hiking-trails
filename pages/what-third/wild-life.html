Wild life

The wild animals and plats is the citizens of the park. They are in charge of our visit. It means that we are not aloud to disturb the animals, break branches or pick flowers. The nature also provides attractions. There is a lot to see i the park. Here are some example.

Mouflon

This sheep is originally from the Caspian region and has been introduced for the sake of hunting. Now its spread in our parks, which is a hunting forbidden area. The big boomerang horn is sigificant for the male.

Great Crested Grebe

This is a common grebe which lives in eutrophic lakes and coasts with reeds. The offspring use to take a ride on the parents back.

Striped Bug

A common and distinct bug in our southern parks in summer time. They does'nt exist in the north. It is easy to find this bug on the plants branches and leaf and they are often more than one at the time.

Sword-leaved Helleborine

This plant is not common anymore and decreasing. But in our southern park there is a lot of them in a few places. You can find them mostly in the deciduous forest where they can grow i bigger groups.

Eurasian Eagle-owl

The Eagle owl is the largest owl in the world. Its not common but you can here them calling in the dusk and dawn in the mounting area of our parks. They are protected where they are.

Red-belted Conk

This conk is common and likes especially dead spruces. The red, yellow and grey color are easy to see in the forest.Its used to be collected for its tinder and capability to make fire.

Common Crane

The crane is common in spring when arriving from spain to Sweden. They dancing and calling a lot in the fields. In the summer they have a are more secret behavior when they are breeding.

Yellowhammer

The yellowhammer lives here the whole year, but part of the population is migrating. The like shrubs where they breed and take cover in. You can hear the song when they count to seven.

Silver-washed Fritillary

This day flying butterfly is common and can be seeing in larger groups. They like warmer areas with flowers and nearby deciduous trees.

Tawny Owl

Tawny owl is common in the south and rare in the north. They are a typical night owl which calls in the dark. In may there is a chance to see the juvenile on the branches at day time when learning to fly.

Apollo

Apollo is a large day active butterfly which lives in specific areas width limestones. They are flying mostly in july and are not shy when sucks nectar. The red and white dots with a black border are distinct for the Apollo.

Glacier Buttercup

This Buttercup lives up to 2000 meter in the mountain up in the north. The are growing in small groups or solo. They can only be seeing in a few places in our trails.
